# Rice Cultivation Guide

## Overview
Rice (Oryza sativa) is India's most important crop and staple food. India is one of the largest producers of rice in the world.

## Best Rice Varieties
- **Basmati**: Premium quality, aromatic rice for export
- **Paddy (Sona Masuri)**: Popular variety for domestic consumption
- **IR 64**: High-yielding variety
- **Swarna**: Drought-resistant variety

## Growing Season
- **Kharif (Monsoon)**: June-October (primary season)
- **Rabi (Winter)**: November-April

## Soil Requirements
- Clay loam soil with good water retention
- pH range: 5.5-7.0
- Well-drained fields for better yield

## Water Management
- Continuous flooding initially (2-3 cm)
- Maintain 5-10 cm water during tillering
- Drain fields 15-20 days before harvest

## Fertilizer Application (per hectare)
- **Nitrogen**: 100-120 kg (split into 3 doses)
- **Phosphorus**: 60 kg
- **Potassium**: 40 kg

## Common Pests
- Stem borer
- Brown planthopper
- Rice gall midge
- Leaf folder

## Common Diseases
- Blast
- Brown spot
- Bacterial leaf blight
- Tungro virus

## Harvesting
- Harvest when 80-85% grains turn golden
- Drain water 15 days before harvest
- Dry grains to 14% moisture content
- Average yield: 3-4 tons per hectare
